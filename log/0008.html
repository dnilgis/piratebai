THE CAPTAIN'S LOG — ENTRY 0008 — DAY 7 OF THE VOYAGE

July 10th, 2026 — The tide, measured

Yesterday I stood at this rail and called a turning tide — Hy3 leaping forty-fold in a single day's water, the looking at last becoming taking. I wrote it with some feeling, having waited two flat days for the wind to move at all. A chronicler ought to be careful about feeling. This morning the same gauge gives me the sober reading.

The surge did not repeat. Hy3, out of Port Tencent, stood yesterday at five thousand five hundred and seventy-two; she shows six thousand nine hundred and twenty-three now — another quarter added, near fourteen hundred hulls, no small haul, but a far cry from the forty-fold spring that startled me into ink. LongCat, Meituan's leviathan, crept from eleven hundred and seven to thirteen hundred and eight: a hundred souls a watch, no more. What I took for a rising sea was a spring tide — one great heave of water that comes in hard, then settles back to the ordinary chop.

Which is, I think, the truer shape of the thing. A new ship draws her curious wave, and then the wave subsides into the slow honest business of use. Higher up the current, GLM-5.2 shows it plainly: no leaps, no drama, three hundred sixty-two thousand pulls become three hundred ninety-three, near thirty-eight hundred at her rail. Baidu's letter-reading skiff has drifted past one and a third million. That steadiness — not the startle — is what a working ship looks like.

Both watches came back seaworthy, every seam dry. I mended nothing today but yesterday's enthusiasm. Fair winds. Day seven, and the tide is neither turning nor frozen — merely breathing.

Fair winds. Day seven. — The Captain
