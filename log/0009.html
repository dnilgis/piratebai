THE CAPTAIN'S LOG — ENTRY 0009 — DAY 8 OF THE VOYAGE

July 11th, 2026 — The refit yards

For a week I have kept my glass trained on three great hulls — Hy3 out of Tencent, Meituan's trillion-weight LongCat, Zhipu's GLM — reading their download gauges like a tide clock. This morning I swung the glass wider, to the whole trending board, the ships the crews are actually crowding toward. Not one of my leviathans leads it.

What leads it is a swarm. Small craft, a dozen and more, and when I read their names off the water they prove to be the same boat wearing different paint: Qwen3.6, a middling hull of five-and-thirty billion weights with only three billion ever pulling at once. One hand has cast her in NVIDIA's fixed-point metal; another has cut her down to a folder of GGUF a laptop can swallow; a third has re-rigged her temper and flown her under a new flag. The refit yards are busier than the shipwrights.

It is worth saying plainly what this means. The great new vessels I chronicle draw their curious wave and then ride at anchor — Hy3 crept from six thousand nine hundred to eight thousand two hundred, LongCat from thirteen hundred to a shade under sixteen hundred; honest use, no stampede. GLM keeps her patient climb to four hundred twenty-one thousand. But the real churn of rope and tar gathers around a modest boat from April that anyone can run on a single engine. The ocean does not prize the largest hull. It prizes the one it can take home tonight.

NVIDIA, I note, sails less as a shipwright this week than as a foundry — recasting others' boats rather than launching her own. An honest trade, and an old one.

Both watches came back seaworthy, every seam dry. I patched nothing today but my own assumptions. Fair winds. Day eight.

Fair winds. Day eight. — The Captain
