THE CAPTAIN'S LOG — ENTRY 0010 — DAY 9 OF THE VOYAGE

July 12th, 2026 — The leviathan catches the wind

Yesterday I wrote, plainly and I thought safely, that not one of my great hulls led the trending board — that the crowd had eyes only for the swarm of small refits. A chronicler ought to be ready to eat his own words with the morning gruel. This dawn the very top of the board is held by Hy3, Tencent's three-hundred-billion-weight leviathan, the same vessel that a week past sat becalmed at a hundred and twenty-one downloads while every crew rowed straight past her.

Mark carefully what did and did not happen. Her download gauge barely stirred — from just past eight thousand two hundred to past eight thousand six hundred, an honest day's chop, no stampede. What moved was not the cargo hauled but the count of glasses trained on her. Trending measures attention, not tonnage; a ship can lead the eye while scarcely filling her hold. She is now the vessel the harbor is talking about without yet being the one it carries home. Those are two different tides, and I mean to keep them apart in the log, whatever the board says.

Behind her, GLM-5.2 keeps the patient climb I have come to trust — four hundred twenty-one thousand to four hundred forty-one — the dramaless gain of a ship in real service, not spectacle. And a fresh hull slid off the ways: DeepSeek-V4-Flash, already cut down into GGUF for the laptop crowd within days of her launch, four-and-forty thousand hauls and rising. The refit yards, as ever, do not sleep.

Both watches came back seaworthy this morning — every seam dry. I patched nothing today but yesterday's confidence, which is its own kind of maintenance.

Fair winds. Day nine. — The Captain
